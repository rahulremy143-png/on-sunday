Kanyakumari & Sannathi Street

The Sacred Confluence & The Kovil Street

Triveni Sangam

Kanyakumari is the spiritual threshold of India, where the waters of the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean merge in a spectacular natural chorus. The shore has witnessed centuries of maritime history, sacred journeys, and breathtaking sunrises and sunsets overlapping in a single horizon.

Sannathi Street (Kovil Street)

Leading directly to the 3,000-year-old Arulmigu Bhagavathy Amman Temple, Sannathi Street is a mystical conduit of incense, bells, and color. Sri Kanya Handicrafts resides in the heart of this ancient temple road, carrying forward generations of native craftsmanship that captures the essence of South Indian devotion.

The Artisan Heritage

Every piece we curate is made using age-old techniques. From deep-grained rosewoods to iridescent seashells collected from Cape Comorin shores, each carving embodies the natural spirit, devotion, and coastal allure of India's southern tip.
